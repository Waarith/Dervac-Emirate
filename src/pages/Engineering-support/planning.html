Engineering Facilities

Engineering Planning & IT Systems consists of the following four departments:

Asset Life Cycle Management

ALCM is responsible for ensuring compliance with regulatory authority regulations from the point a new
aircraft is delivered to Emirates, right the way through to when that aircraft is returned to the organisation
that owns it (Lessor). It does this by ensuring it maintains the highest standard of data quality and aircraft
records. It is responsible for the inputting of technical data, the transaction of components replaced on
each aircraft, digitally scanning every single sheet of documentation, and making it available electronically,
for the rest of the organisation to use and refer to.

It is responsible for ensuring the continued airworthiness of the Emirates fleet and it does this by
constantly auditing all the data it retains, producing and verifying reports that are used for production of
Airworthiness Release Certificates for each aircraft. It also verifies every single transaction conducted, has
the responsibility of managing landing gear overhaul programs and ensuring engines returning from shop
visits will meet intended return conditions when handed back to the owners. It also manages the return of
the aircraft/engines at the end of their Lease periods. A dedicated Team ensures that every return
condition, set out in the Lease agreement, is fulfilled by Emirates without financial penalty.

ALCM personnel work 365 days of the year. They are highly skilled and they play a pivotal role in ensuring
continued airworthiness is maintained right across the fleet of EK aircraft.

Operational Planning

The Operational Planning section consists of Aircraft Scheduling, Line Planning, Line Support Planning, Base
Operations Planning, Base Heavy Planning and Projects teams.

Operational Planning are responsible for ensuring available aircraft ground-time in direct coordination with
work scoping, planning functions and processes and packaging control for all scheduled maintenance
ground-time for its maintenance teams in Line Maintenance, Line Support Maintenance, Base Operations
and Base Heavy Maintenance with the major functions:

  • Aircraft Scheduling:

    • Determine long-range, medium-range and short range plans, steer budget decisions and dictate the
    ground time required to accomplish maintenance work, with the minimum disruption to the flight
    schedule. It plays a vital role in planning maintenance slots with Line maintenance,
    Base maintenance and third parties for all Emirates fleet.

  • Work scoping:

    • Responsible for the definition of scheduled work packages which produce consistent packages of work
    to the production departments to allow accurate forward planning of hangar workspace and the
    associated manpower requirements to complete all maintenance required at the optimum time. Review,
    plan and work scope maintenance requirements for execution, balance scheduled work load within
    given maintenance capacity constraints along with timely issuance and control of work packages to
    maintenance teams.

  • Modification Planning:

    • Provide vital planning and support services so that maintenance teams can carry out smooth, effective
    maintenance and repair on the Emirates fleet, ensuring minimum aircraft ground time, undisrupted
    workflow and optimum turnaround time. This can be as simple as adding a new model of soap
    dispenser to the lavatories or completing major structural improvements to sections of the aircraft that
    have been found susceptible to fatigue or age related structural defects that may have been discovered
    during the life of the aircraft. Liaison with manufacturer’s working parties for modification support is
    also a vital function of this cell. Liaison with the Materials Management Section to ensure parts and tools
    required to support the modification campaign are planned to be available to meet the modification
    timeline.

  • Special Projects:

    • Support for specific activities such as the workscoping of End of Lease aircraft workpackages and
    management of outsourced aircraft maintenance and the planning and management of aircraft in
    parking or storage condition.

    Reliability

    The main purpose of the Emirates Reliability Program is to establish and continuously monitor the
    maintenance program effectiveness for the fleet of Emirates aircraft and through continual monitoring
    provides case evidence to trigger investigations in adverse trends and/or support the effectiveness of the
    maintenance program during escalation or de-escalation investigations by maintenance planning.

    Technical and Reliability data pertaining to aircraft operations is gathered, analysed and periodically
    presented in consolidated Reliability Reports enabling technical decisions to improve aircraft safety,
    reliability and to reduce maintenance cost. Ensures adherence to all applicable civil aviation regulations.
    Manage and monitor ETOPS, RVSM and Autoland reliability. Lead the Fleet Reliability Working Group
    meetings including setting the agenda and deciding topics for discussion. Recommend actions to Fleet
    Managers, Fleet Engineers and Maintenance Program Manager based on reliability data analysis and
    insights for the purposes of optimizing the maintenance program. Reviews all reliability action item
    responses and validates the response is acceptable prior to closing.

    Responsible towards development of reliability metrics to be tracked across the fleet for the purposes of
    measuring, tracking and comparing fleet reliability. Accountable for identifying reliability trends and
    emerging reliability issues for awareness and action by the Fleet Manager and Engineering teams.
