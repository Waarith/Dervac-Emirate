Engineering Technical Services

The Engineering Technical Services (ETS) department provides fleet technical management, continuing airworthiness management and technical support functions for one of the most technologically advanced fleets of aircraft in the world, ensuring it operates at the highest level of safety, reliability, cost effectiveness and passenger comfort.

The engineers and technical staff in this department engage actively with aircraft manufacturers and vendors to drive the development of new aircraft technologies and systems. Engineering Technical Services includes Fleet Technical Management, GCAA/EASA Design Organization, Maintenance Programmes & Configuration and Technical Publications.

Fleet Technical Management

Fleet Technical Management consists of five specialist groups to ensure the Emirates Fleet operates to the highest standards of service and technical despatch reliability.

  • Airframe Systems

Provides services for the mechanical aspects of airframe systems and components. This includes flight controls, air conditioning system, windows, doors (mechanical), landing gear, hydraulics, pneumatics, fuel, cargo, loading, fire extinguishing, water, waste systems, electrical and software controls for components relating to airframes.

  • Structures

Provides services in support of metallic and composite aircraft structures - this includes passenger doors, flight control surfaces, landing gear structural items and doors, baggage/cargo provisions and exterior markings. In collaboration with manufacturers, the section also develops and approves structural repairs for aircraft and components and liaises with manufacturers to undertake repairs of a minor nature that are not covered by repair manuals.

  • Powerplant

Provides services for power plant and APU systems and accessories, including engine health and APU condition monitoring, optimisation of engine time on-wing, engine removal planning and work scoping, monitoring of borescope inspection findings.

  • Cabin

Provides fleet technical services for passenger cabin and flight deck. This includes passenger & flight deck seats, galleys, toilets, stowage structures, interior markings, carpets & curtains, emergency & medical equipment, galley equipment (e.g. trolleys, ovens, coffee makers, boilers) and our state-of-the-art on-board bars, shower spas and lounges. The cabin team also manages cabin retrofit projects and re-configurations.

Design Organisation

  • UAE GCAA & EASA PART-21 Design Organisation

Under the authority of the UAE GCAA DOA approval (GCAA.21J.001) and EASA Part 21 DOA approval (EASA.21J.103), the Engineering Technical Services department is authorised to carry out changes to the approved type design and technical data associated with aircraft or components, as specified in the approval. The approvals cover the development of approved data in the form of minor changes to type design, modifications, and minor repairs through a design assurance system. The Design Organisation is an integral part of Emirates Engineering, its personnel are located in within the Fleet Technical Management department where they perform a technical support function for the airline, in addition to their design activities.

Maintenance Programmes & Configuration

The Maintenance Programmes and Configuration department comprises three specialist sections: Fleet Maintenance Programmes, Fleet Modification Programmes and Fleet Configuration Management.

  • Fleet Maintenance Programmes

    • Manages the definition of Aircraft Maintenance Programme under UAE GCAA CAR M 302. The Aircraft Maintenance Programme is derived through recommendations from the aircraft, engine and APU manufactures along with in-service experience and requirements mandated by Airworthiness Authorities.

    Once the programme is defined and approved, the requirements are fed into our enterprise software for maintenance forecast and compliance control, based on fleet utilisation. The Aircraft and Component/Engine level Maintenance Programmes are managed by different sub-sections under very close coordination and provide input for scheduled maintenance work package scoping for the hangar and in-house work-shops.

  • Fleet Modification Programmes

    • Manages aircraft modification programme definition and control for accomplishment on fleet aircraft and components.

    The modification programme definition team coordinates initial spare and tool provisioning to establish achievable modification schedules in collaboration with key stakeholders. Modification control definition ensures appropriate management of dependencies i.e. subsequent, concurrent or superseding relationships.

    The modification programme team also ensure communication with aircraft manufacturers for required updates to technical publications which may have been affected due modifications.

  • Fleet Configuration Management

    • Ensures effective configuration management of fleet aircraft and their components through our enterprise software.

    The baseline configurations are derived from aircraft delivery document data and enhanced by our own operational requirements. Configuration management maintains the traceability of controlled components by location on the aircraft and associated required information e.g., usage, modification status etc. supporting Supply Chain, Reliability Monitoring and Lease Returns.

    Technical Publications Centre

    Technical Publications Centre (TPC) is part of the Engineering Technical Services department. TPC receives, controls and disseminates (publishing and distribution) engineering, maintenance and operational technical data to support Emirates Engineering and Operations worldwide. TPC documentation services support Emirates approvals in accordance with UAE GCAA CAR M, CAR 145, CAR 21 and CAR-OPS 1.
